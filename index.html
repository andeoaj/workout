FAQ

Your questions, answered.

What is executive coaching?
Executive coaching is a confidential, one-to-one developmental process designed to help professionals enhance their performance, navigate challenges, and achieve meaningful goals. It is forward-looking and action-oriented, distinct from therapy or mentoring.
Why are the sessions free?
I am completing my ICF certification, which requires a documented number of coaching hours with real clients. Offering free sessions allows me to accumulate those hours while providing genuine value to participants. The quality of coaching is the same as a paid engagement.
What happens in a coaching session?
Each session is tailored to you. We typically begin by setting the agenda for that conversation, then explore the topic using coaching techniques — powerful questions, reflection, and structured inquiry. Sessions are 45–60 minutes and held online or in person.
Is what I share confidential?
Absolutely. Everything discussed in our sessions is strictly confidential. I adhere to the ICF Code of Ethics, which mandates full confidentiality. The only exception would be if there were a risk to your safety or that of others.
Do I need to be an executive to apply?
Not at all. The term "executive coaching" refers to the methodology and professional context, not a job title requirement. If you are a professional with goals, challenges, or transitions you want to work through, coaching can be valuable at any level.
How do I know if we are a good fit?
We start with a brief chemistry call — no commitment, no pressure. If we both feel there is a good fit, we proceed. If not, that is completely fine. Good coaching depends on a genuine connection.
